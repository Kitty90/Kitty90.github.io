Kruidkoek or ontbijtkoek is a very traditional Dutch cake. When I was young, my mom used to bake something special on Sundays. Her kruidkoek was one of my favorites and this is my adaptation of hers. The traditional recipe calls for milk and special Dutch ingredients such as koek- en speculaaskruiden (Dutch spice blend) and Dutch basterdsuiker (made with sugar, sugar syrup, and caramel or molasses), but these can easily be replaced with other spice blends, demarara sugar and almond milk.

In the Netherlands koek- and speculaaskruiden is a common spice blend for baking, especially for winter cookies (speculaas, pepernoten). Note that it is different from Belgian speculoos, which is not a spice blend, but a cookie made with molasses / caramelized sugar. Speculoos actually means the cookie is spiceless. Speculoos was created in Belgium in the early 20th century because the traditional speculaas spices were very expensive in Belgium; unlike the Netherlands Belgium didn't have easy access to spices via its colonies in the East Indies.

It's interesting that countries all have different spice blends for baking. Here are some varieties, and their ingredients. They might be somewhat different, but all can be used for kruidkoek if you don't have speculaaskruiden.

  • Dutch speculaaskruiden: nutmeg, cinnamon, aniseed, coriander seed, cardamom, clove, ginger, white pepper
  • British mixed spice: cinnamon, allspice, nutmeg, cloves, ginger, coriander seed, mace
  • German gingerbread (Lebkuchen) spice:cinnamon, allspice, nutmeg, cloves, ginger, coriander seed, mace, cardamom, star anise
  • Swedish gingersnap (pepparkakor) blend: cinnamon, cloves, ginger, cardamom
  • American pumpkin pie spice: cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, cloves, allspice

Recipe: Vegan Dutch Spice Cake

Ingredients (one loaf)

  • 220 g wheat flour
    •
  • 250 g of light brown sugar (Dutch: lichte basterdsuiker)
    •
  • 80g almond meal
    •
  • 15 g baking powder
    •
  • 4 tsp pumpkin spice / mixed spice / gingerbread spice (I used Dutch koek- en speculaaskruiden)
    •
  • a pinch of salt
    •
  • 500 ml of almond milk
    •
  • 200 g of mixed nuts, chopped
    •

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 175 C. Grease the cake tin with some oil.
  2. Mix all the dry ingredients in a large bowl.
  3. Add the almond milk and mix carefully. Add the nuts and mix them through but do not overmix the batter.
  4. Transfer the batter to the baking tin.
  5. Bake for 30-35 minutes, until nicely browned and a toothpick comes out clean.
  6. Let the cake cool down for 10 minutes before taking it out of the tin.
