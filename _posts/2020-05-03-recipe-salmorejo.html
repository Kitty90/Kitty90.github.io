The time has come for summer recipes! First up: Salmorejo, a chilled tomato soup that can easily be made vegan or vegetarian.


Salmorejo is a very simple chilled soup from Córdoba, Andalucía (southern Spain), that I like to call 'blended breakfast soup'. That might not sound very appealing, but the first time I was preparing it, I realized that Salmorejo consists of the same ingredients as the typical Spanish breakfast Pan con tomate (toasted bread with grated tomatoes, garlic, olive oil and ham). "Bless the Spanish", I thought. They have not only succeeded in inventing a simple treat for breakfast with these ingredients, their climate has probably inspired them to combine the same  flavors in a chilled soup. Just the recipe we need to kick off summer!

Recipe: Salmorejo


Ingredients ( serves4)

  • 1 kg (35 oz) tomatoes
    •
  • 1 clove of garlic
    •
  • 200 g (7 oz) stale bread, shredded into smaller bits
    •
  • 150 ml (2/3 cup) extra virgin olive oil
    •
  • optional: 4 hard boiled eggs (leave out to veganize the recipe)
    •
  • 1 green bell pepper
    •
  • optional: 2 slices of jamón Serrano (leave out to veganize the recipe)
    •

Method

  1. To dry the Serrano ham, pre-heat the oven to 160 C / 325 F and put the slices on an oven tray covered with baking paper.
  2. To peel the tomatoes, put a generous amount of water in a pot (enough to submerse the tomatoes) and bring to boil.
  3. Cut tiny crosses in the bottoms of the tomatoes. When the water is boiling, add the tomatoes and leave in the pot for 30 seconds.
  4. After 30 seconds, take out the tomatoes with a soup ladle, place them in a colander and rinse with cold water. Leave to cool down a bit.
  5. Meanwhile, put the tray with Serrano ham slices into the oven. Check regularly, the ham is done when it is dry, crispy and breaks when bent.
  6. Starting at the edges of the crosses, peel the skin of the tomatoes and put them in a bowl, blender or kitchen machine.
  7. Add the clove of garlic to the bowl and blend the tomatoes until the mixture has a smooth, frothy texture.
  8. Add the stale bread by the handfuls, mixing the bread into the soup after adding each handful.
  9. After adding the bread, add the olive oil and mix thoroughly.
  10. Let the soup chill in the refrigerator and take the ham out of the oven, if it is inflexible and crispy.
  11. While the soup is cooling down, prepare the garnish. Cut the green pepper into small cubes, hard-boil two eggs (min. 8 min boiling time) and cut them in halves, and break the ham slices into shards.
  12. When chilled, stir the soup again to mix the substance properly and ladle into bowls.
  13. Garnish the soup with two halves of hard-boiled eggs, some green pepper and ham shards.
