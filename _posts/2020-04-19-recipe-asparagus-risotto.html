What I mean to say is: Let's talk about asparagus.

The white asparagus, to be more precise. After some due research I discovered that, in many countries, green asparagus are common across the globe and the white asparagus is quite rare. In the Netherlands we can get both, although I prefer the white variety, probably because their seasonal availability has increased my appreciation for them. Oh, who am I kidding? Despite the fact that the only difference between the varieties is that the white was raised as a vampire, deprived of any sunlight, I believe that they simply taste stronger while having a softer texture.

Now, about the recipe. I know that asparagus are not exactly budget friendly and some of you might therefore consider my processing this 'king of vegetables' into cream a capital offence. However, I assure you, I promised you an ode in the title of this post, and that is exactly what this recipe delivers. I have used every bit of the asparagus to infuse the risotto and make it the star of the dish. The result? A rich, creamy and herby risotto, that honors the asparagus and allows you to enjoy the distinct note of the white gold in every single bite.

Recipe: White Asparagus Risotto

Ingredients (serves 4)

  • 320 gr / 1.6 cups of Arborio rice
  • 500 g / 1 lb 2 oz fresh white asparagus
  • 125 g / 1/2 cup of  mascarpone cheese
  • 8 - 12 slices prosciutto di Parma (leave out for vegetarian option)
  • 1 onion
  • 40 g / 0.2 cup of butter
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • Parmigiano reggiano, for grating
  • 1 vegetable stock cube
  • bunch of parsley
  • ground pepper
  • salt

Method

  1. Put a large pot with ample water on the fire. There should be enough water in the pot to submerge 3/4 of the asparagus in upright position. Add a generous amount of salt and bring water to boil.
  2. Cut the woody bottoms of the asparagus, about 1 cm (1/2 inch).
  3. To remove the woody outer skin, peel the asparagus with an asparagus peeler (or a potato peeler). Tie the asparagus together with a string. Set the asparagus peels and bottoms aside for the broth.
  4. Put the asparagus bunch in upright position in the pot with boiling water. Leave to cook for 20 minutes.
  5. Meanwhile, chop the onion very finely and put it in a bowl.
  6. Put 20 g of butter and 2 tablespoons of extra virgin olive oil in another bowl.
  7. After 20 minutes of cooking, take the asparagus out and leave to cool down in a colander.
  8. Keep 1 l of the cooking liquid and add the stock cube and asparagus peels and bottoms. Bring this broth to a boil and let it simmer.
  9. Cut 4 cm chunks from the top of the asparagus. These asparagus tips are for garnish.
  10. Cut the remaining asparagus in chunks. These will be used to make the asparagus cream.
  11. Put all of the chunks (asparagus tips included) in the bowl with olive oil and butter let the asparagus soak up the liquid.
  12. Once the asparagus have absorbed the liquid, add one ladle of the broth and leave this to be absorbed as well.
  13. Meanwhile, toast the Arborio rice briefly and add it to the onion bowl.
  14. Once the asparagus have absorbed the stock, take out the asparagus tips and set aside.
  15. Add the mascarpone to the asparagus chunks and mix well, so the asparagus seem to be swimming in yogurt.
  16. Blend the asparagus mixture with a handmixer until they are completely pureed and you are left with a silky, creamy asparagus sauce.
  17. Add a lug of olive oil to a sauce pan on medium heat and add the rice and onion. Add a ladleful of stock to the rice and bring to a boil.
  18. When the risotto is boiling, turn the heat down to a simmer, and let the rice absorb the stock, stirring continuously. Keep adding ladlefuls of stock to the rice, allowing each ladleful to be absorbed before adding the next.
  19. After about 10 minutes, add the asparagus cream to the rice and cook the rice to finish (about 5 minutes). Add the remaining 20 g of butter if the rice is cooked. If the rice is not cooked yet, you can add some more stock.
  20. Roll up the asparagus tips in the slices of ham.
  21. Chop a handful of parsley and add half to the risotto and bring it to taste with ground pepper.
  22. Scoop the risotto onto plates. Put 2 or 3 asparagus-prosciutto roll-ups on each serving. Finally, grate over the Parmigiano and sprinkle over the rest of the chopped parsley.
Buon appetito!
